Yacht Charter Market Overview

The global yacht charter market reached $15.2 billion in 2025 and is projected to grow 5-7% annually through 2030. Post-pandemic interest in private, outdoor travel experiences continues to drive demand, with first-time charterers making up an estimated 35% of bookings in 2025.

Chartering a yacht is more accessible than most people think. Bareboat sailboat charters start at approximately $1,500 per week in shoulder season, which splits to under $200 per person per day for a group of eight. Crewed catamaran charters with all-inclusive meals and service start around $10,000-$15,000 per week, comparable to a luxury resort stay when divided among guests.

The most popular charter types in 2026 are bareboat (you sail the yacht yourself), skippered (a professional captain handles navigation while you relax), crewed (full crew including chef), and cabin charter (book individual cabins on a shared vessel, similar to a cruise but more intimate).

Greek Islands

Why Charter Here

Greece remains the world's most popular yacht charter destination, and for good reason. Over 6,000 islands and islets, the Cyclades' iconic white-and-blue architecture, crystal-clear Aegean waters, and reliable summer winds create the perfect sailing environment. The Ionian Sea offers calmer conditions ideal for beginners, while the Aegean provides more challenging sailing for experienced crews.

Best Routes

The Cyclades route (Mykonos, Paros, Naxos, Santorini) covers the iconic islands over 7-10 days. The Dodecanese route (Rhodes, Kos, Symi, Nisyros) is less crowded with excellent historical sites. The Ionian route (Corfu, Paxos, Lefkada, Ithaca, Kefalonia) offers protected waters and lush green landscapes.

Pricing

Bareboat: $2,000-$4,500/week in shoulder season; $3,500-$8,000/week in peak (July-August). Marina fees average $30-80 per night. Provisioning for a week costs approximately $200-300 per person. Greece charges a 12% cruising tax included in charter fees since 2024.

Croatia and the Adriatic

Why Charter Here

Croatia has become the Mediterranean's fastest-growing charter destination. Over 1,200 islands along the Dalmatian coast, medieval walled towns like Dubrovnik and Split, excellent seafood, and increasingly sophisticated marina infrastructure make it a compelling choice. The sailing is relatively straightforward with good wind patterns and short distances between islands.

Best Routes

The Split to Dubrovnik route covers Hvar, Korcula, Mljet National Park, and the Elaphiti Islands. The Split to Sibenik route visits Brac, Vis (one of Europe's best-preserved islands), and Kornati National Park's lunar landscape of 89 uninhabited islands.

Pricing

Bareboat: $1,800-$4,000/week shoulder season; $3,000-$7,000/week peak season. Croatia is slightly more affordable than Greece overall, with lower marina fees averaging $20-60 per night. A skipper license (ICC or SCI) is strictly enforced in Croatia.

British Virgin Islands

Why Charter Here

The BVI is often called the "sailing capital of the world" for good reason. Consistent trade winds of 15-20 knots, short distances between islands (most are visible from one to the next), well-protected anchorages, and clear warm water make navigation easy and enjoyable. The Baths at Virgin Gorda, with their massive granite boulders creating natural swimming grottos, are a must-visit.

Best Routes

A classic 7-day BVI loop from Tortola visits Norman Island (the inspiration for Treasure Island), Peter Island, Cooper Island, Virgin Gorda, Anegada (the only coral island), and Jost Van Dyke (home to the famous Soggy Dollar Bar). The entire territory covers a compact area that minimizes long open-water passages.

Pricing

Bareboat: $3,000-$6,000/week shoulder season; $5,000-$10,000/week peak season (December-March). National park mooring fees are $30 per night. The BVI charges a $4 per person per day environmental levy. Provisioning costs are higher than Mediterranean destinations at $300-400 per person per week due to island pricing.

The Bahamas

Why Charter Here

The Bahamas offers the closest exotic sailing to the US mainland, with 700 islands spread over 100,000 square miles of turquoise water. The Exuma Cays are the crown jewel, featuring the famous swimming pigs at Big Major Cay, the Thunderball Grotto (a James Bond film location), and some of the clearest water on Earth with visibility exceeding 200 feet.

Best Routes

The Exuma Cays route from Nassau heads south through the Exuma Cays Land and Sea Park, Staniel Cay, Big Major Cay, and the pristine southern Exumas. The Abacos route offers protected barrier reef sailing with charming colonial settlements. For experienced sailors, the Out Islands route visits Eleuthera, Cat Island, and Long Island.

Pricing

Bareboat: $3,500-$7,000/week in peak season. Crewed catamaran charters all-inclusive from $15,000-$25,000/week. Bahamas cruising permits cost $300 for boats up to 35 feet and $500 for larger vessels. Fuel costs are notably higher than Mediterranean destinations.

Thailand

Why Charter Here

Thailand offers exceptional value in the yacht charter market, with stunning limestone karsts in Phang Nga Bay, pristine islands in the Andaman Sea, and world-class cuisine at every stop. The sailing season runs November through April, with consistent northeast monsoon winds providing reliable sailing conditions. Infrastructure has improved significantly with modern marinas in Phuket and Koh Samui.

Best Routes

The Phuket to Langkawi route heads south through the Phi Phi Islands, Koh Lanta, and Koh Lipe before crossing into Malaysia. The Phang Nga Bay circuit from Phuket explores the dramatic limestone karsts, sea caves, and hidden lagoons made famous by "The Beach" and James Bond films.

Pricing

Bareboat: $1,500-$3,500/week, making Thailand one of the most affordable charter destinations globally. Crewed charters start at $8,000/week all-inclusive. Local food costs are remarkably low at $10-20 per person per day eating at island restaurants. Marina fees average $15-30 per night.

Sardinia and Corsica

Why Charter Here

The Sardinia-Corsica circuit is the Mediterranean's luxury sailing destination. The Costa Smeralda in northeast Sardinia features some of the world's most exclusive marinas and restaurants. Corsica offers dramatic mountain-meets-sea scenery, excellent hiking, and authentic French cuisine. The Strait of Bonifacio between the two islands provides exciting sailing with reliable winds.

Best Routes

The Costa Smeralda loop from Olbia covers Porto Cervo, the Maddalena Archipelago, Bonifacio (Corsica), and the stunning pink-sand beaches of Budelli. A Corsican circumnavigation from Ajaccio visits Bonifacio, Porto-Vecchio, Bastia, and the Scandola Nature Reserve.

Pricing

Bareboat: $3,000-$6,000/week shoulder; $5,000-$12,000/week peak. This is among the most expensive Mediterranean charter regions. Marina fees in Porto Cervo can exceed $200/night in August. However, shoulder season (May-June, September-October) offers 30-40% savings with excellent weather.

Seychelles

Why Charter Here

The Seychelles offer an unmatched combination of tropical beauty and sailing convenience. The 115 granite and coral islands are compact enough for a week-long charter while diverse enough to fill a month. Giant granite boulders frame white sand beaches, giant tortoises roam freely, and the marine life is extraordinary. The sailing conditions are moderate year-round, with no hurricanes.

Best Routes

The Inner Islands route from Mahe visits Praslin (home to the Vallee de Mai and the endemic Coco de Mer palm), La Digue (possibly the world's most photographed beach, Anse Source d'Argent), Curieuse, and the smaller granitic islands. Snorkeling and diving are exceptional throughout.

Pricing

Bareboat: $3,000-$5,500/week. Crewed catamaran charters from $12,000-$20,000/week all-inclusive. National park entrance fees apply at certain islands ($25-30 per person). The Seychelles has no sailing license requirement, though charter companies verify competence. Flight costs to reach the Seychelles add to overall trip expense.

Booking Tips and Best Practices

  • Book 6-12 months ahead for peak season (December-March for Caribbean, July-August for Mediterranean). Early booking secures the best boats and prices.
  • Consider shoulder seasons. May-June and September-October in the Med offer 20-40% lower prices, fewer crowds, and still-excellent weather.
  • Compare charter brokers. Sites like Getmyboat, Click&Boat, SamBoat, and The Moorings aggregate listings. Prices vary by up to 20% for the same boat across platforms.
  • Factor in extras. Fuel, provisioning, marina fees, tips (10-15% on crewed charters), insurance damage deposits ($1,000-$5,000 refundable), and cruising permits all add to base charter cost.
  • Split costs with friends. A $4,000/week catamaran split 8 ways costs $500 per person per week, less than most hotel vacations.
  • Check cancellation policies. Most charter companies offer full refunds with 30-60 days notice. Charter insurance (3-5% of charter price) protects against unexpected cancellations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to charter a yacht?

Bareboat charter prices range from $1,500 to $5,000 per week for a 35-45 foot sailboat. Crewed yacht charters start at $10,000 per week for smaller vessels and can exceed $200,000 per week for luxury superyachts. Cabin charter options where you book individual cabins start at $1,000-$2,000 per person per week.

Do I need a license to charter a yacht?

For bareboat charters, most charter companies require proof of sailing competence such as an ASA certification, RYA Day Skipper, or ICC. Some companies accept a detailed sailing resume. Crewed charters require no license since professional crew handles all sailing. In Croatia and some other countries, a local license may be required.

What is the best time of year to charter a yacht?

Mediterranean charters are best from May to October, with July and August being peak season. Caribbean charters run November to April, with December to February being peak. Shoulder seasons (May-June and September-October in the Med; November and April in the Caribbean) offer lower prices and fewer crowds.

What is included in a yacht charter price?

Bareboat charters typically include the yacht, basic equipment, and insurance. You pay extra for fuel, provisioning, marina fees, and cleaning. Crewed charters usually include crew, meals, some drinks, water toys, and fuel. The APA (Advance Provisioning Allowance) of 20-35% covers onboard expenses for crewed charters.

Can beginners charter a yacht?

Yes. Beginners have several options: book a crewed charter where professionals handle everything, join a flotilla (guided group sailing) that provides instruction along the way, or take a week-long learn-to-sail course before chartering. Cabin charters on crewed catamarans are the easiest entry point for complete beginners.
